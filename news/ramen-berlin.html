Berlin's New Ramen Wave: Why the Capital Now Has the Ultimate Noodle House

WhatsApp Twitter / X

Berlin is going through a ramen revolution. In the last four weeks, three new restaurants have opened that focus exclusively on Japanese noodle soups — and all three use hand-pulled noodles and broths that simmer for up to 18 hours.

Fukagawa Ramen — Prenzlauer Berg

The smallest of the three sits on Lychener Straße with only 16 seats. The tonkotsu broth is creamier than anything we've tried in Berlin. The owner learned his craft in Fukuoka and brought the original recipe with him. Recommendation: Spicy Miso Ramen with extra chashu.

Cocolo Ramen — Kreuzberg

Cocolo opens its second Berlin location on Paul-Lincke-Ufer. The Shinjuku-style ramen here are lighter than Fukagawa's but more aromatic. The eggs are perfect — soft, juicy, soy-marinated. Tip: come early, weekend wait times are long.

Wen Cheng Ramen — Friedrichshain

The big surprise: Wen Cheng, known for its Biang Biang noodles, has set up a ramen pop-up corner. The fusion ramen with Chinese influences is unlike anything else. The Dan-Dan Ramen with Sichuan pepper is a must for adventurous eaters.

Our Verdict

Berlin now has three addresses that can hold their own against Tokyo. All three are worth it — but if you can only pick one: Fukagawa for authenticity, Cocolo for atmosphere, Wen Cheng for innovation.
