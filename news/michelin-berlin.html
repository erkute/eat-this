Berlin Now Has 8 Michelin-Starred Restaurants — And None Cost More Than €150

WhatsApp Twitter / X

The new Michelin Guide Berlin 2026 edition is out — and it sends a clear message: Berlin is the most democratic fine dining city in Europe. None of the 8 starred restaurants charges more than €150 for a full dinner menu.

The Newcomer

The biggest surprise is Natura, which only opened last year. René Redzepi's Berlin outpost received a star immediately — at a menu price of just €89. Critics speak of revolutionary sustainability on the plate.

The Consistent Ones

CODA Dessert Dining defends its star with a 7-course dessert tasting for €95. Otto in Mitte offers a seasonal 5-course menu for €78 — one of the most affordable Michelin stars in Germany.

Why Berlin?

The answer is simple: lower rents than Munich or Hamburg, an international chef scene, and diners who are willing to pay for quality — but not for status. Berlin fine dining is not a status symbol; it's lived conviction.
