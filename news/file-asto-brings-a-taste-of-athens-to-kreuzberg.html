File Asto Brings a Taste of Athens to Kreuzberg

WhatsApp Twitter / X

File Asto, the Greek‑inspired restaurant that already made itself popular in Kollwitzkiez, has opened a second home in Kreuzberg at Reichenberger Straße 125. The move signals more than just expansion, it feels like a deliberate invitation to slower evenings, shared tables, and easygoing food.

Rather than chasing hype, the concept leans on what already works: uncomplicated dishes, homemade feel, and a focus on ingredients rather than theatrics. It’s the kind of place that seems born to host long dinners with friends, not Instagram‑driven flash‑ins.

Shared Plates, Shared Vibes

At File Asto Kreuzberg, the menu is built around small plates and mezze meant to be shared. Think warm dips, grilled vegetables, simple salads, and a rotating selection of grilled fish or meat depending on the season.

The drink list follows the same logic: wine from the tap, local beer, ouzo, tsipouro, and maybe a little lemon or soda on the side. The idea is not to complicate things, but to let olive oil, salt, oregano, and fresh ingredients do the talking.

A Taste of Athens in Berlin

While File Asto doesn’t try to recreate a full‑blown Greek taverna, it borrows just enough of that spirit to feel transportive. The vibe is unpretentious, the service relaxed, and the lighting forgiving, exactly what you want from a neighborhood spot that also happens to cook very well.

If you’re looking for a place where you can linger over a few plates, a bottle of wine, and some conversation, Kreuzberg’s new File Asto fits the bill. It’s the kind of restaurant that feels at home serving a quiet Tuesday evening as much as a birthday‑dinner‑on‑a‑Sunday.
